Portfolio Pillars

Four worlds, one production language.

The environments are intentionally broad enough to show range, but each shares the same underlying practice: stylized shaders, readable traversal, modular dressing, and romantic editorial presentation.

World 01

Sakura Dream

桜の夢 / sakura no yume

Petal drift, lacquered wood, gentle route framing, soft bloom, and a shrine-axis composition built around Torii, Sando, Kairo, and Haiden.

  • Hero route render with readable foreground, midground, and destination.
  • PCG heatmap plus beauty shot showing route-safe dressing.
  • Nikki-style sheen, sparkle, and pastel material comparison.
World 02

Space Cathedral

星の聖堂 / hoshi no seidou

Celestial arches, stained light, NASA starmap-driven material work, parallax nebula surfaces, and monumental fashion-fantasy scale.

  • Wide nave composition with silhouette and cosmic ceiling read.
  • MI_Show_CelestialNebula shader plate with starmap source visible.
  • Emissive trims and constellation navigation motifs.
World 03

Baroque Castle

バロックの城 / barokku no shiro

Gilded modular ornament, velvet contrast, impossible perspective, and Escher-inspired pattern logic carried through trims and material families.

  • Castle hall render with ornamental route cues and impossible-depth framing.
  • MI_Baroque_EscherOrnament close-up material plate.
  • Trim sheet breakdown connecting filigree to modular kit pieces.
World 04

Bioluminescent Grotto

光の洞窟 / hikari no doukutsu

Glowing moss, translucent water, layered stone, procedural scatter, and calm discovery lighting for an intimate fantasy environment.

  • Low-light cave hero render with water reflection and moss glow.
  • Material plate for wet stone, emissive moss, and translucent water.
  • Geometry/procedural dressing breakdown for rock and foliage clusters.

Shared Systems

What every world needs to prove.

The portfolio gets stronger when each environment shows both mood and method. These proof categories should repeat across the four pillars.

01

Traversal Read

Each hero render should show a route, destination, scale cue, and visual reward.

02

Shader Identity

Every world needs a material family with one beauty plate and one graph or instance proof.

03

Modular Logic

Trims, meshes, scattering, or procedural rules should explain how the world can scale.

04

Editorial Finish

The final page should feel like a fashion artbook: refined labels, restrained motion, and clear art direction.

05

Escher Thread

Tessellation, recursion, topology, and impossible perspective should appear where they serve the scene.

06

Capture Contract

New renders should flow through the capture brief, Unreal intake, validation, and GitHub Pages publish path.
