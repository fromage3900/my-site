Impossible Systems / Escher Logic

The Escher brain is part of the charm.

I keep coming back to M. C. Escher because his work makes math feel playful instead of sterile. Tessellation, recursion, symmetry, topology, and perspective play all map directly into how I think about environment art: modular layout, shader masks, retopology, procedural dressing, and worlds that feel handcrafted even when the system is quietly helping.

Tessellation

Repeating motifs for trims, flooring, foliage cards, and decorative shader masks.

Topology

Clean deformation logic, retopo discipline, and surfaces that support lighting instead of fighting it.

Recursion

Small motifs reappearing at world, prop, material, and UI scale for a cohesive fantasy read.

Perspective Play

Compositions that feel magical while still giving the player a readable route through the scene.
